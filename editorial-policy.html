Content Principles

The Lab Care Research Team operates according to a set of explicit content principles. These principles govern everything from how we select topics to how we handle claims that turn out to be wrong. This page makes those principles visible so readers can evaluate the information they find here with full context.

Principle 1: Distinguish Evidence from Consensus

Not all claims are equal. Some things about Labrador care are established by controlled research. Others are widely accepted in practice without strong formal evidence. Others are genuinely contested. The Lab Care Research Team applies one of these three labels — implicitly or explicitly — to every health claim it makes. We do not present accepted practice as proven fact, and we do not treat contested claims as settled.

Principle 2: Flag Uncertainty Rather Than Resolve It

Some areas of Labrador care — the optimal feeding frequency to reduce bloat risk is one example — involve genuine uncertainty in the literature. Our standard is to describe that uncertainty clearly rather than to select a position because it sounds more authoritative. Lab owners making real decisions about their dog's care deserve to know when the evidence is limited.

Principle 3: Use AI Tools Responsibly

The Research Team uses AI tools in the drafting and research aggregation stage of content production. No AI-generated draft is published without substantive review and editing by a human member of the Research Team. On health-sensitive topics — particularly bloat, joint conditions, and nutritional disorders — AI output is treated as a starting framework, not a finished source.

Principle 4: Correct Errors Transparently

When a published guide contains an error, the correction is made to the live page and noted in an update stamp. We do not quietly edit out incorrect information without acknowledging that it was wrong. Where a correction is material to a health decision, it is flagged prominently. Reader corrections are reviewed by the Research Team and acted on when the evidence supports revision.

Principle 5: Commercial Independence

Affiliate income supports this site's operation. The full details are on our Transparency Note page. The Research Team's editorial judgements — which topics to cover, what positions to take, what products to mention — are made without reference to revenue implications. Accuracy is the only metric that matters for editorial decisions.

Submitting a Correction

The Contact page is the right channel for corrections. Include the article URL, the specific claim, and ideally a source that supports the revision. The Research Team reviews all submissions.
